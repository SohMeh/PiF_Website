Our Mission

Pay it Forward Inc. (PiF) is an Illinois-based nonprofit dedicated to democratizing STEM education and research. Our mission is to empower K–8 students through hands-on workshops, research-based curricula, and international partnerships that spark scientific curiosity and build a foundation for future innovation.


We break down barriers by developing innovative, accessible methodologies that create opportunities for every student—regardless of background or resources. Our programs are designed not only to educate but also to inspire, keeping pace with modern scientific and technological advancements.


A unique aspect of our approach is our team of passionate high school seniors. They combine their individual research advances to deliver a novel, research-based approach to teaching that makes STEM education accessible, engaging, and impactful for all students.


As our impact grows, we reinvest in communities—locally and globally—by channeling donations and funding back into programs that further enhance STEM learning. In this way, we truly "pay it forward."

Monthly STEM Workshops

Within the Bloomington-Normal area, we hold local hands-on workshops that serve as teaching initiatives employing hands-on curricula designed to inspire curiosity and build confidence in young learners.

Our workshops cover a wide range of STEM topics, making complex concepts accessible and engaging for K-8 students. Each workshop is carefully designed to promote active learning and scientific inquiry.

Notably, we've hosted Physics, Biology, Anatomy, and Engineering workshops, reaching over 1,000 people locally alone. Our dedicated team of high school seniors brings their research experience and passion to each event, creating memorable learning experiences.

Workshops are typically held monthly and are open to all students, regardless of background or prior experience in STEM. We provide all necessary materials and ensure that our activities are age-appropriate and engaging.

Uganda Partnership

Our partnership in Uganda helps children with limited access to education receive responsible and empowering STEM instruction. We've been privileged enough to engage in this partnership through Sir Bamutiina Gregory, who runs an NGO and advocates for equity within Africa.

Through this collaboration, we provide educational resources, curriculum materials, and virtual mentoring to support STEM education in underserved communities. Our goal is to inspire scientific curiosity and build foundations for future innovation among students who might otherwise lack access to quality STEM education.

This partnership exemplifies our commitment to global impact and educational equity. By sharing resources and expertise across borders, we're helping to democratize STEM education on an international scale.

Research-Based Teaching

We apply our team's collective research advances to develop innovative teaching methods that make STEM accessible to all learners. Our approach combines the latest educational research with practical classroom techniques to create engaging, effective learning experiences.

Each member of our team brings their unique research expertise, allowing us to develop a multidisciplinary approach to STEM education. By incorporating findings from cognitive science, educational psychology, and specific STEM disciplines, we create curricula that address diverse learning styles and needs.

Our teaching methodologies focus on hands-on exploration, critical thinking, and real-world applications. We believe that effective STEM education goes beyond memorization to foster genuine curiosity and problem-solving skills.

A full publication and metrics of our literature review will be publicly available soon! This comprehensive resource will provide educators and researchers with insights into our evidence-based approaches and their outcomes.
